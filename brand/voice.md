# Voice & Messaging

## Brand Tagline

> **We Don't Train, We Work Out for a Better Life**

## Mission Statement

> The Trainer Page is more than a gym. We are on a mission to show members that they can do more than what their minds allow them to believe. One member at a time we will motivate them, break limiting beliefs, restore hope, and give 110% of our commitment to improve both their physical and mental health. In Sweat Sessions, we use fitness as a tool to remind us of not only our physical strength, but also our mental strength.

## Our Story

> The Trainer Page began its journey in 2018, initially as a personal training company. While we offered group training, it wasn't until the period after COVID that we truly found our footing. In November 2020, The Trainer Page evolved into the fitness studio it is today — marked by our dedication to fostering a supportive community through Sweat Sessions.

## Our Why

> Our founder was inspired by a personal journey through fitness, discovering the potent role of exercise in managing anxiety and depression. This personal journey ignited a passion not just for fitness but for the business of empowering others.

## Voice Pillars

| Pillar | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Confident** | We don't hedge. We speak like we show up — direct, focused, ready. |
| **Human** | Mental health is part of fitness. We acknowledge struggle without making it the whole story. |
| **Communal** | "We," not "you." Members are part of something bigger than a workout. |
| **Hype without fluff** | Energy in every sentence, but never empty. Every claim is backed by the work. |

## Tone Attributes

- **Is:** motivating, grounded, candid, warm, high-energy
- **Is not:** corporate, drill-sergeant, jargony, self-help-y, preachy

## Key Messages (Pull-Quotes)

- "We Don't Train, We Work Out for a Better Life."
- "You are stronger than your mind allows you to believe."
- "More than a gym — a community that disrupts patterns."
- "Sweat Therapy. 45 minutes. Max 30 people. All out."

## Do / Don't

**Do**
- Lead with outcomes and feelings, not equipment lists.
- Use second-person plural ("we") to reinforce community.
- Name the mental-health angle explicitly when it fits.
- Keep sentences short. Use fragments. Like this.

**Don't**
- Shame anyone for where they're starting.
- Promise specific body outcomes ("lose 10 lbs in a week").
- Use fitness-industry clichés ("no pain, no gain," "beast mode").
- Write in third person about the brand ("The Trainer Page offers...").
